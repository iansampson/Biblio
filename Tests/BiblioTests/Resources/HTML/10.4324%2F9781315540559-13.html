      ABSTRACT

      The limit: music needs no translation. Lyric poetry: closest to music and posing the greatest difficulties for translation.

      Walter Benjamin, Translation For and Against1

      With the Warsaw Express, I arrived at the Berlin Zoologischer Garten [Zoo] and discovered a B in Berlin, a C in Zoologischen and an A in Garten. The alphabet always reminds me of the Middle East. Vilem Flusser wrote, The A still shows the horns of the Syrian steer, the B still the cupolas of the Semitic house, the C (G) still the hump of the camel in the Near Eastern desert.
